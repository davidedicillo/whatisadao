What is a DAO?

A DAO, or decentralized autonomous organization, is a type of decentralized network that is run by a set of rules encoded as smart contracts on a blockchain platform.

DAOs are often used to create decentralized networks of people who share a common goal or purpose, such as a community of artists, developers, or investors. By using smart contracts, DAOs can automate many of the processes and decision-making within the network, making them efficient and transparent.

DAOs can be used for a wide range of purposes, such as creating decentralized markets, voting systems, and governance models. They are a key component of the broader decentralized finance (DeFi) movement, which aims to provide financial services on blockchain platforms in a decentralized manner.

Overall, DAOs are a powerful tool for creating decentralized networks and enabling new forms of organization and collaboration on the blockchain.
